II. Subject Pronouns

  • yo – I
  • tú – you (informal)
  • usted – you (formal)
  • él / ella – he / she
  • nosotros / nosotras – we (masculine / feminine)
  • vosotros / vosotras – you all (informal, Spain only)
  • ustedes – you all (formal)
  • ellos / ellas – they (masculine / feminine)

Note: Use tú for informal situations (friends, family) and usted for formal ones (teachers, strangers).

III. The Verb Ser (To Be)

Used for:

  • Identity – Ella es Claudia.
  • Profession, origin, nationality – Roberto es de Chile.
  • Descriptions – El hotel es elegante.

Present Tense Conjugation:

  • yo soy – I am
  • tú eres – you are
  • él/ella/usted es – he/she/you (formal) is
  • nosotros/nosotras somos – we are
  • vosotros/vosotras sois – you all (informal, Spain) are
  • ustedes/ellos/ellas son – you all / they are

IV. Cognates

Words that look and mean the same in both languages.

  • Normal – normal
  • Hotel – hotel
  • Activo – active
  • Responsable – responsible
  • Excelente – excellent

False Cognates: Be careful with words like:

  • Largo – means "long", not "large"
  • Colegio – means "high school", not "college"

V. Adjectives

Adjectives must match the gender and number of the noun.

Examples:

  • viejo / vieja – old (masc./fem. singular)
  • viejos / viejas – old (masc./fem. plural)
  • responsable / responsables – responsible (sing./plural)
  • pesimista / pesimistas – pessimistic (sing./plural)
